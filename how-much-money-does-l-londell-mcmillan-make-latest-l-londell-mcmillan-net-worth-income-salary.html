How Much Money Does L. Londell McMillan Make? Latest L. Londell McMillan Net Worth Income Salary

L. Londell McMillan (born 1966 in Bedford–Stuyvesant, Brooklyn, New York) is an American entertainment lawyer, producer, and publisher who works in the entertainment industry. He graduated from Brooklyn Technical High School in 1983 and went on to get his bachelor’s degree from Cornell University, where he was a member of the Quill and Dagger Society, an honours graduate, and an Academic All-American football player, among other accolades. The New York University School of Law was the next stop for McMillan after graduating from Cornell University in 2008. McMillan was a member of the Practicing Law Institute’s faculty, as well as a speaker at the Harvard Business School and a prominent case study topic there.

He has also appeared as a guest and pundit on many television shows, including The Oprah Winfrey Show, The Today Show (NBC), Entertainment Tonight, and CNN. McMillan was named one of The National Law Journal’s 50 Most Influential Minority Lawyers in America in 2008, and he was also named one of New York’s Super Lawyers in 2007 and 2008. In 2001, he received the Haywood W. Burns, Lawyer of the Year Award from the Metropolitan Black Bar Association, and he was featured on the cover of Black Enterprise Magazine in December 2003. 

Crain’s Business named McMillan as one of the “100 most powerful minority leaders in the country.” In his previous position as a partner and co-head of the Media and Entertainment Global Industry Sector Group at the international law firm of Dewey & LeBoeuf LLP, he worked on a variety of media and entertainment matters. Before his death, his clientele included Prince, Lil’ Kim, Stevie Wonder, Chaka Khan, and Michael Jackson, to name a few celebrities. The Discovery Channel, Time Warner, Screenvision, Radio One, the National Football League, and Mercedes-Benz are just a few of the companies that have worked with him on business projects.
