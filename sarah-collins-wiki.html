Who is ‘The Voice’ Sarah Collins? Wiki, Bio

Sarah Collins gained prominence after appearing on the reality singing show ‘The Voice.’ Plus, she also created an impression on two coaches.

Ms. Collins is named after the “Sara” song of the Fleetwood Mac. She hails from Baton Rouge. Later, she moved to Dandridge after graduation. She’s had a regular gig at Ole Red Gatlinburg since then, according to a press release.

She’s recently been named the month’s Ole Red artist and has plenty of shows lined up for March including one at 8 p.m. on March 5.

Sarah Collins said when she doesn’t sing — she spends her days writing from her balcony and with her family tubing on the lake. She also said that she draws inspiration from the beautiful views of the Smokies.

At the age of 8, Ms. Collins began guitar and voice lessons and has performed in musical theatre and school events. She began high school homeschooling to focus on her music and opened for Hunter Hayes and Kane Brown.

‘The Voice’: Sarah Collins‘ Blind Audition’

Following her performance, Ms. Collins received feedback from the coaches. She said that the two coaches – Legend and Shelton – had some issues with a pitch while singing.

One of the coaches, Shelton said,

I’m sorry I didn’t turn around,

I always get excited when a country artist auditions for the show. I’m with John though. I just feel like there were enough pitch problems that I didn’t hit my button, but who cares? You’re on ‘The Voice.’

After the feedback, Ms. Collins went on to choose Kelly Clarkson as her mentor.
