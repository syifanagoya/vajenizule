Early Life & Biography

Alan Menken was born on the 22nd of July in 1949 at a French hospital which is located in New York City. He is the son of Judith and Norman Menken. His father was not only a dentist but also a boogie-woogie piano player while his mother was a dancer, an actress, and a playwright. This can explain where Alan Menken got his musical talent.

Alan Menken studied at New Rochelle High School, which is located in New Rochelle, in New York. He graduated in 1967 and enrolled at New York University, where he majored in Musicology. He graduated with a degree from the University’s Steinhardt School in 1971. He said that he was interested in medicine, and he thought he would take after his father and become a dentist.

Although he took a degree in Musicology, he says he had no or very little interest in music until he joined the BMI workshop. He then enrolled in the BMI Lehman Engel Musical Theatre Workshop after he graduated from University. This marked the beginning of his career as a singer and composer.

Career

Alan Menken is a successful composer, music conductor, songwriter, director, and record producer. He is famously known for his songs and scores for films that were produced by Walt Disney Animation Studios. Some of the films he has done scores for include; The Little Mermaid (1989), Pocahontas (1995), Beauty and the Beast (1991), and Aladdin (1992)have all each won Alan Menken two Academy Awards.

He also the one who was responsible for composing the songs and scores for Newsies (1992), Little Shop of Horrors (1982), Sister Act (2009), and A Christmas Carol in 1994. Apart from composing songs and scores for different films, he has also collaborated with great lyricists. Some of these lyricists include; Howard Ashman, Lynn Ahrens, Tim Rice, Jack Fredman, Stephen Schwartz, Glenn Slater, and David Zippel, among others. With eight Academy Award wins, Menken has been marked the 2nd most prolific Oscar winner in the category of music after Alfred Newman, who has received a total of 9 Oscars.
